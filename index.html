Weekly Tea-Time Discussions

Week 1: Chasing Ghosts - Neutrino Oscillations and Reactor Physics

Discussion lead: Nuclear Physics Group | April 10, 2026

This week's tea time took a turn into the subatomic. We discussed the latest in neutrino oscillation experiments, the immense challenges of detecting these non-interacting particles, and their broader implications for the Standard Model and nuclear reactor monitoring.

Week 2: Order in the Squish - The Physics of Soft Matter

Discussion lead: Condensed Matter Team | April 3, 2026

Stepping away from rigid crystalline lattices, we explored the fascinating world of soft condensed matter. The conversation covered the viscoelastic behavior of polymers, the phase transitions of liquid crystals, and how thermal fluctuations dominate the mechanics of these easily deformable systems.

Week 3: Dislocation Dynamics in BCC Refractory High-Entropy Alloys

Discussion lead: Materials Science Group | March 27, 2026

A deep dive into the stress-strain response of complex multi-principal element alloys. We analyzed how severe lattice distortion in refractory HEAs affects dislocation movement during uniaxial tension and compression, backing up experimental observations with molecular dynamics simulation data.

Week 4: Pushing the Limits - High-Strain-Rate Testing with SHPB

Discussion lead: Experimental Mechanics | March 20, 2026

We examined the dynamic behavior of dense metals like Tungsten Heavy Alloys under extreme impact. The chat focused on the nuances of capturing and analyzing Split Hopkinson Pressure Bar (SHPB) data, specifically looking at thermal softening and the initiation of adiabatic shear banding at high temperatures.
